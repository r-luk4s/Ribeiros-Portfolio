Who am I?

I'm someone who turns chaos into structure without ever killing the emotion. As a programmer and visual creator, I build things that need to feel. My work is shaped by intensity — I'm not interested in making what already exists. I bring fragments of who I am into every line of code, every element on screen. Melancholy, noise, harmony — it's all part of it.

What moves me?

Music is at the center of everything. It's not just background noise — it's a pulse that drives my ideas. I'm into striking visuals, dark aesthetics, and bold experiments. I don't care for safe or conventional — if it doesn't push a limit, it doesn't belong in my projects — that's my world.

How do I work?

With precision, but never cold. I care about detail like a composer cares about silence. Every interaction should mean something. I chase flow, atmosphere, and identity. My work is built to reflect contradiction: discipline and disarray, softness and aggression. Not just code. Not just design. A piece of me.
